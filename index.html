Rules and Regulations

Rules of Procedure

Rules of procedure give an overview of the most important rules MUN simulations should follow. In simple words, MUNs follow certain rules called Rules of Procedure (ROPs) in order to facilitate debate.

Roll Call:

  • At the beginning of every session, the chairperson shall call in an alphabetical order on all Member States in order to state their status of attendance.
  • Status of attendance: Member States may reply “present” or “present and voting”. Those representatives who choose the second status shall have no right to abstain from substantial voting.

Quorum:

  • Quorum is the minimum number of delegates required to open the meeting and for the GA to take any decision.
  • The quorum for opening a GA meeting is when at least 1/3rd the Member States are present.
  • The quorum for the adoption of resolutions/decisions is a simple majority of Member States (½ +1 votes required to pass).

Debate:

There are two modes of debate in an MUN:

  • Formal Debate: Includes General Speaker’s List (GSL).
  • Informal Debate: Includes Moderated Caucus and Unmoderated Caucus.

Voting:

There are two types of voting:

  • Procedural Voting: No abstentions allowed; all observers have the right to vote. Requires simple majority (½ +1 votes).
  • Substantial Voting: Used for resolutions, amendments, or documents. Requires a 2/3rd majority to pass.

Glossary & Important Terms:

  • Decorum: Order in committee.
  • Roll Call: The Chair may call on each State and ask them to state their vote.
  • Speaker’s List: A list that contains the order of speakers in the committee.
  • Motion: A specific action made by delegates to direct debate in a specific direction.
  • Adjournment of Debate: It ends specific parts of the debate, such as discussions on a Draft Resolution.
